Statutory Instruments

2011 No. 1656

Highways, England

Olympic Games And Paralympic Games

The Olympic Route Network Designation (Amendment) Order 2011

Made

6th July 2011

Laid before Parliament

11th July 2011

Coming into force

8th August 2011

The Olympic Delivery Authority, with the consent of the Secretary of State for Transport, makes the following Order in exercise of the power conferred by section 11(2) of the London Olympic Games and Paralympic Games Act 2006(1).

In accordance with section 11(4)(a) of that Act, the Olympic Delivery Authority has consulted the relevant authorities referred to in that section.

Citation and commencement

1. This Order may be cited as the Olympic Route Network Designation (Amendment) Order 2011 and comes into force on 8th August 2011.

Amendment of Order

2.-(1) The Olympic Route Network Designation Order 2009(2) is amended as follows.

(2) In the Schedule (roads designated as roads in the Olympic Route Network)-

(a)Part 1 is amended in accordance with Schedule 1 to this Order; and

(b)Part 2 is amended in accordance with Schedule 2 to this Order.

Signed by authority of the Olympic Delivery Authority

Hugh Sumner

Director of Transport

Olympic Delivery Authority

EXPLANATORY NOTE

(This note is not part of the Order)

This Order, which is made by the Olympic Delivery Authority ("the ODA") with the consent of the Secretary of State for Transport, amends the Olympic Route Network Designation Order 2009 ("the 2009 Order"). The amendments make additions and changes (including the removal of certain roads) to the list of roads set out in the 2009 Order which constitute the Olympic Route Network.

An impact assessment of the effect that this instrument will have on the costs of business and the voluntary sector is available from the Olympic Delivery Authority at One Churchill Place, Canary Wharf, London, E14 5LN and is annexed to the Explanatory Memorandum which is available alongside the instrument on the OPSI website (www.legislation.gov.uk). Copies of each document have been placed in the library of each House of Parliament and may also be found by visiting the ODA's website at www.london2012.com/olympic-route-network.
