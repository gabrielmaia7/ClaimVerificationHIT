Task Overview

In this task, we want your help verifying whether a collection of 5 passages extracted from a website, each containing some highlighted text, either support, refute or are inconclusive to an affirmation. You will be asked to do this 6 times during this task.

Each time, you will receive an English affirmation (such as 'Obama was president of the US') and a set of 5 passages containing highlighted text extracted from a website (such as a BBC News article). Your goal is to tell us if, when taken into consideration together the highlighted parts of the passages support, refute, or are inconclusive to the affirmation.

You will be asked to judge how the 5 highlighted texts together relate to the affirmation by selecting from the following four options: Support, Refute, Neither (they are inconclusive), and Not Sure. In case you select Not Sure we will ask you to select a reason from a predefined list.

If your reason for selecting Not Sure is Other, a little box will appear for you to tell us what it was. Please provide a simple reason. It helps us improve this task to cost you and others less time to complete!

Please note:

  • We are not asking if the affirmation is true. Even if the affirmation is potentially or provably false, or goes against some common sense, please judge only if the highlighted text in the passage supports it, refutes it, or neither.
  • The passages might not naturally read like a paragraph. They are automatically extracted from a website, so they might have strange formatting, such as a lack of punctuation and incomplete phrases. Please do not let this lack of structure impact your judgement much. We recommend clicking on the website's URL to see what the passages look like originally and what information the highlighted texts are meant to say. If it still is impossible to understand how the highlighted texts relate to the affirmation, select Not Sure.
  • Please only judge the highlighted texts. For example, if another portion of the passage that is not highlighted supports the affirmation, but the highlighted text does not support it, do not take its support into consideration.

Examples

Example #1

Affirmation: Vidyasagar is a performer of Ponniyin Selvan.
Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ponniyin_Selvan_(2005_film)&oldid=982538956

  • Passage 1:
      "15 August 2005 ( 2005-08-15 ) Country India Language Tamil Budget ₹ 10 crore. Ponniyin Selvan (English: The son of Ponni) is a 2005 Indian Tamil drama film written and directed by Radha Mohan. Produced by A. M. Ratnam, it stars his son Ravi Krishna, along with Gopika and Revathi in the lead roles, while Vidyasagar had composed the music for the film. The film is unrelated to the same-titled novel by Kalki Krishnamurthy. It was released in Telugu as Muddhula Koduku and additionally featured Uttej and Venu Madhav as part of a separate comedy track shot."
  • Passage 2:
      "Ponniyin Selvan (English: The son of Ponni) is a 2005 Indian Tamil drama film written and directed by Radha Mohan. Produced by A. M. Ratnam, it stars his son Ravi Krishna, along with Gopika and Revathi in the lead roles, while Vidyasagar had composed the music for the film. The film is unrelated to the same-titled novel by Kalki Krishnamurthy. It was released in Telugu as Muddhula Koduku and additionally featured Uttej and Venu Madhav as part of a separate comedy track shot."
  • Passage 3:
      "Ponniyin Selvan Soundtrack album by Vidyasagar Released 2005 Genre Soundtrack Label Five Star Audio Producer Vidyasagar Vidyasagar chronology. Chandrolsavam (2005) Ponniyin Selvan (2005) London (2005) "
  • Passage 4:
      " Categories: 2005 films Tamil-language films Indian films 2000s Tamil-language films Tamil films scored by Vidyasagar Indian drama films 2005 drama films Films directed by Radha Mohan 2000s Tamil-language film stubs Hidden categories: Articles with short description Short description matches Wikidata Use dmy dates from November 2015 Use Indian English from November 2015 All Wikipedia articles written in Indian English Template film date with 1 release date Pages using infobox film with image size parameter Articles containing"
  • Passage 5:
      "Disclaimers. Contact Wikipedia. Mobile view. Developers. Statistics."

The affirmation states that an entity named Vidyasagar is a performer of something called Ponniyin Selvan. From the link (and also by reading the passages), we can see Vidyasagar is a musician and Ponniyin Selvan is a movie. The first passage's highlighted text directly supports that Vidyasagar composed the movie's music. The second passage's highlighted text only says "Vidyasagar had composed the music for the film", but it is clear by the surrounding text that the movie in question is Ponniyin Selvan. The third passage's highlighted text shows Vidyasagar's album chronology, including Ponniyin Selvan. The fourth appears to list the categories of the original page, including "films scored by Vidyasagar". Lastly, passage 5 seems to be unrelated. Taken together, these highlighted texts seem to Support the affirmation.

Example #2

Affirmation: The work period (start) of MOG Music is 2013.
Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOG_Music&oldid=930246276

  • Passage 1:
      "2.1 Live albums. 3 Awards and nomination. 4 References. Music career. MOG Music released his first album in 2016 "New Wine" which earned him African Gospel Music awards Nominations for "Album of the Year" and "Discovery of the Year". He released a single "Making it Big" featuring Sarkodie. He has collaborated and performed with numerous gospel musicians, including Ohemaa Mercy, Joe Mettle, Denzel Prempeh, Jekalyn Carr, Danny Nettey, Nii Okai, Ron Kenoly."
  • Passage 2:
      "Jump to search. Ghanaian singer. MOG Music Birth name Nana Yaw Boakye Genres Gospel Occupation(s) Singer, songwriter Years active 2013–present Associated acts."
  • Passage 3:
      "Gospel Singer Nana Yaw Boakye Rebrands To M.O.G GhanaNdwom.net."
  • Passage 4:
      "Retrieved 7 November 2019. M.O.G presents New Wine Concert 2016. ghanaweb.com. Retrieved 7 November 2019."
  • Passage 5:
      "Main page Contents Current events Random article About Wikipedia Contact us Donate. Contribute. Help Learn to edit Community portal Recent changes Upload file.
The affirmation states that an entity named MOG Music started working in 2013. From the link and passages, we can see MOG Music is a musician. The starting of the first passage seems to be headers taken from the website indicating the existence of a music career, with the highlighted part stating the first album was released in 2016. If we assume the first release is the start of their work, this Refutes the affirmation. The second passage's highlighted portion has general info about MOG Music and a non-highlighted portion saying they are active since 2013. However, since this is not highlighted, we do not consider it! The third, fourth, and fifth passages do not have relevant information in their highlighted parts. Taken together, these highlighted texts seem to Refute the affirmation, as one highlighted text refutes it and the rest are unrelated.

Affirmation: Julius is 53.1 centimetres tall.
Link: https://www.rct.uk/collection/402419/julius

  • Passage 1:

Twitter. Email."
  • Passage 2:
      "'Home. / Collection. / Explore the Collection. / Julius / Search the collection."
  • Passage 3:
      "Davis belonged to a family of distinguished Royal huntsman; he was encouraged to paint animals by George III. After training with Sir Francis Bourgeois and at the RA schools he became Animal Painter to George IV, William IV and Queen Victoria. Nothing is known of the horse beyond his name, 'Julius'"
  • Passage 4:
      "Medium and techniques. Oil on canvas. oil paint, lined canvas. Measurements. 53.1 x 65.4 x 1.5 cm (support, canvas/panel/str external) 69.8 x 85.1 x 5.8 cm (frame, external) Groupings. Category. Paintings."
  • Passage 5:
      "Main page Contents Current events Random article About Wikipedia Contact us Donate. Contribute. Help Learn to edit Community portal Recent changes Upload file.
The affirmation states Julius is 53.1 cm tall, which is a weird height for a person. Reading the passages indicates "Julius" is a painting, which can be confirmed by clicking on the URL. The first highlighted text directly states the dimensions of the painting, including 53.1 cm. Checking the URL helps us conclude that 53.1 cm indeed refers to the height of the painting (since it is wider than it is tall, and 1.5cm is too small). Passages 2, 3, and 5 have no relevant highlighted text. The highlighted text of passage 4 also lists the measurements of the painting. Taken together, since two support and three are unrelated, the highlighted texts Support the affirmation. On some such cases, where a deduction is needed (the first dimension probably refers to height), we also accept Not Sure, given an appropriate reason.


Neither

In cases where all five highlighted texts are unrelated to the affirmation, are too vague to conclude, or contradict each other, you can select Neither. For instance, consider example #3 if passages 1 and 4 had highlighted the start of the passage instead. No relevant text would have been highlighted and Neither would be the response. Consider also example #2 if the ending of passage 2 was highlighted. There would be conflicting highlighted information, and you could mark either Support, Refute, or Neither, depending on your personal opinion of what constitutes "starting work".

Not Sure

Sometimes, affirmations might not be very clear or have words and terms that are too specific to one knowledge domain (like biology or chemistry). Also, the passages might have a very unregular format, and even looking at the website does not help. For any case where you legitimately can not arrive at a conclusion, you can mark Not Sure, provide a fitting reason, and move on.

Task rules

  • You can navigate freely through the affirmation-passages sets using the buttons at the bottom of the page.
  • You must fill in all the information requested. This means always clicking either Support, Refute, Neither, or Not Sure, and providing a reason if you selected Not Sure. This also means providing another reason if you chose Other.
  • At the end of the task, we check some indicators of the quality of your work, to make sure you are not a spammer. If you don't pass the test, you can try two more times, for a total of three trials. If you fail three times, the task ends and the work is not accepted.
  • This task will need you to have a good comprehension of English. Please answer the following short English language test and click on the green button to proceed. You can try the language test three times.
  • You can open these instructions again anytime you wish.
  • Make sure you are using either Chrome, Firefox, or Edge. Do not use Internet Explorer.
  • If you suspect there is an error in the task, or think we are being unfair, contact us politely and we will do our best to resolve the issue.
